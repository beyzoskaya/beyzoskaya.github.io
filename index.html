Research Interests

My work lies at the intersection of bioinformatics, machine learning, chromatin architecture modeling, graph neural networks, and temporal gene expression analysis.

  • Graph-based representations of biological systems
  • Chromatin interactions and Hi-C data modeling
  • Multi-resolution genomic structure prediction
  • Spatio-temporal gene expression prediction
  • Integrating graph features with biological sequence analysis

Projects

1. GAT-HiC — Graph Attention Model for Chromatin Structure Prediction

In collaboration with my advisor Dr. Emre Sefer, I developed GAT-HiC, a graph attention–based model designed to predict unseen chromatin structures from experimental Hi-C contact maps.

  • Leverages graph attention networks (GATs) to model 3D chromatin organization
  • Generalizes across different resolutions, restriction enzymes, and species
  • Captures structural dependencies using biologically informed graph features
  • Designed for cross-setting and cross-species prediction robustness

2. STEP — Spatio-Temporal Expression Prediction

I also developed STEP (Spatio-Temporal-Expression-Prediction), which includes two versions: STEPmr and STEPmi, targeting mRNA-specific and miRNA-specific gene sets.

  • Predicts gene expression values across different time points
  • Uses Hi-C–derived features combined with graph representations of interacting genes
  • Integrates temporal interactions from mRNA and miRNA time-series datasets
  • Captures dynamic relationships between chromatin structure and gene activity

Both projects aim to demonstrate how chromatin architecture and gene interaction graphs can be combined to form powerful models for understanding the regulatory dynamics of the genome.
